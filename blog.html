7/27/19



I have been inspired by my grandpa Charlie’s Autobiography and also Kelsey’s keeping of a journal to start keeping a running narrative of some kind. My grandpa wrote his autobiography in 2000 as an explanation of his life or “so that you may know who your dad is and why I am the way that I am.” He regrets that he “may not have shown or expressed [his] love… as [he] should have.” To rectify this he lays out his life on 40 typed pages that recount war, married life, his professional life and money struggles and health issues. He did well for himself, studied accounting when he got back from the war, took night classes to get ahead, built his own home in Page, Arizona and even became Vice Mayor of the small city. He did what he needed to do to provide for his family and if he didn’t show his love to them in words or hugs he did so by living an exemplary life and providing them with opportunities to do this for themselves. On the last page he lists four regrets, number three he says is “Not realizing that Charles Jr. was very sensitive and needed more attention from me.” He doesn’t explain this any further and the only other time he mentions Charles Jr. is to note that he was born. I never met him. He was my mom’s brother and he died of a heart attack when I was young. I only know they called him Fireball because of his red hair. I worry about his heart and why he died so young.

7/28/19



It's 6:51am on a Sunday morning. A little bit later than when I would be getting up on a weekday. I can't sleep in anymore, I need to distract myself with something to keep from worrying about work. I wish that I could still be sleeping! I can't remember dreaming about anything. We are supposed to go to my parent's house today for dinner with my aunt Marie and her husband Mike. My aunt Shiela and my grandma are coming too. We used to have dinner every Sunday at my grandma's house when I was young. We would stay until after the Simpsons was over, back when a new one would be coming out on Sunday evening. I guess the new ones still do come out on Sunday evening but who knows who watches those anymore. Someone must because somehow the show keeps coming out. I think it's the longest running Sitcom in existence. I remember when I was young I thought that if they ever stopped making the Simpsons I would die. I guess things really aren't supposed to last forever, no matter how hard an end can be. We used to play the Simpsons arcade game at the Boardwalk and Chuck E. Cheese. The sidescrolling one where you would beat up people and clowns and rabbit people for some reason as a family. I remember playing it with my mom dad and Amanda at the boardwalk. My mom liked the mini-game where you inflated the hot air balloon by mashing buttongs back and forth. She liked playing games like that. She caught all of the Pokemon in the original game and she had over 1,000,000 Neopoints. It was fun for her and probably a way to distract herself from overthinking, the way I am doing now.
